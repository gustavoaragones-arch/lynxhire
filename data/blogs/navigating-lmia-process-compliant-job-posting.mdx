Hiring a foreign worker through a Labor Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) requires strict adherence to Government of Canada advertising rules. **IRCC recruitment compliance** isn't optional—it's the foundation of a successful application. This guide walks you through the mandatory recruitment phase so your **LMIA job posting requirements** in 2026 are clear, documented, and defensible to Service Canada.

## The 4-Week Rule

Before you apply for an LMIA, you must demonstrate that you tried to fill the position with Canadians or permanent residents. A core requirement: **advertise the position for at least four weeks**. That period is non-negotiable. Service Canada uses it to assess whether the labour market was tested in good faith. Plan your timeline accordingly: start recruitment early, and keep proof of your advertising dates. **LMIA advertising rules** exist to protect Canadian workers and ensure employers only turn to temporary foreign workers when no suitable local candidate was found.

## The Mandatory Triple-Threat — Where to Post

Service Canada typically expects the job to be posted in **three places**. Missing one can delay or derail your LMIA. Here’s the breakdown.

### 1. Job Bank (Government of Canada)

**Job Bank** is the foundational requirement. Your posting must appear on [Job Bank](https://www.jobbank.gc.ca) for the full recruitment period. A proper **Job Bank posting guide** starts with creating or updating your employer account and ensuring your job details match what you use elsewhere.

## Reporting Compliance — "Reason for Not Hiring"

A critical data point for your LMIA application is **why you did not hire** Canadian or permanent resident applicants who applied. Service Canada and IRCC expect you to document that you reviewed applicants fairly and had lawful, job-related reasons for not selecting them.
