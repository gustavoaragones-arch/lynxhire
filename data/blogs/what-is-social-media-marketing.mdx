High‑traffic, low‑trust job sites often look cheap on paper—but their hidden costs are significant. Ghost postings (roles that aren't truly open), fraudulent ads, and misleading descriptions waste candidate time, erode trust in your brand, and attract regulatory attention. With 2026 rules increasingly requiring platforms to maintain written policies and reporting channels for fraudulent ads, the days of "anything goes" classifieds are numbered.

LynxHire positions itself as the premium, scam‑resistant alternative. We recognize that trust is not a banner on your homepage; it's an engineering, policy, and governance choice.

## Why ghost postings and scams are so damaging

Ghost postings—ads kept live to "collect resumes" or test the market—create real harm:

Candidates invest time and emotional energy into roles that never existed or were already filled.

Employers' brands suffer as word spreads that applying to them feels pointless.

Platforms become cluttered with stale listings, making it harder to find genuine opportunities.

Fraudulent postings—used for identity theft, fee scams, or unsafe work—add legal and reputational risk for everyone involved, including the hosting platform. Regulators are increasingly treating job boards as quasi‑labour infrastructure with responsibilities around fraud detection and reporting.

## 2026 expectations for fraud controls

Across Canada, especially in Ontario, legislators are moving toward:

Requiring platforms and employers to publish clear job posting and anti‑fraud policies.

Mandating fraud reporting mechanisms and retention of job posting records for multiple years.

Treating job platforms that enable scams as part of the problem, not neutral intermediaries.

In this environment, platforms that lack verification, audit logs, or active moderation are increasingly risky for reputable employers.

## LynxHire's built‑in protections

LynxHire responds to these risks with concrete features:

A Verified Employer badge based on business identity checks, domain verification, and, where applicable, manual review for high‑risk categories.

A Trust & Safety Policy that explicitly bans fraudulent or misleading postings, supported by reporting tools and enforcement mechanisms.

Immutable audit logs stored in WORM‑style storage, capturing when postings went live, what they contained, and how they were modified, offering defensible evidence if disputes arise.

We also encourage employers to keep postings accurate and current, using tools that make it easy to close roles, update details, and avoid unintentional "ghosting."
